Why Do Raccoons Choose Attics?

Raccoons are intelligent creatures that seek safe, warm spaces for denning, particularly during breeding season. Lexington attics offer several attractive features:

  • Protected environment away from predators
  • Warm, dry space perfect for raising young
  • Easy access to food sources in residential areas
  • Dark and quiet location
  • Multiple escape routes

Signs of Raccoon Activity in Your Attic

Similar to cases we handle in Winchester and Burlington, Lexington homeowners should watch for these telltale signs:

  • Heavy scratching or thumping sounds, especially at night
  • Damaged roof vents or fascia boards
  • Large holes in roof or soffits
  • Raccoon droppings in or around your home
  • Visible paw prints on walls or entry points

Preventing Raccoon Entry

Prevention is crucial, as we discuss in our guide about professional wildlife exclusion. Here are essential steps:

Secure Your Property

  • Install chimney caps and roof vent covers
  • Repair damaged soffits and fascia boards
  • Trim tree branches away from your roof
  • Secure garbage bins with animal-proof lids

Remove Attractants

  • Keep pet food indoors
  • Clean up fallen fruit and birdseed
  • Secure compost bins
  • Remove outdoor water sources

Health Risks and Property Damage

Raccoons can cause significant problems, including:

  • Structural damage to roofs and insulation
  • Risk of raccoon roundworm transmission
  • Contamination through droppings and urine
  • Potential fire hazards from damaged electrical wiring
